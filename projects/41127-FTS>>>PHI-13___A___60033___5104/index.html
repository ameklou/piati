Community-based Emergency Employment and Reconstruction project in Bohol province

1) Support local government to clear damaged structures of debris particularly in vital community infrastructures such as schools, health facilities and critical small community infrastructure by providing emergency employment through Cash-for-Work (CfW) for 22,256 families; 2) Emergency employment to support skills training for construction of temporary shelter and support facilities; 3) Skills training, capacity development and mentoring initiatives to provide alternative livelihood opportunities and ensure the sustainability of these interventions in the medium to long term to revitalize key economic drivers such as agriculture and tourism industry; 4) Provide decent work to vulnerable groups affected by the earthquake in Bohol Provide by observing occupational safety and health and social protection standards.
